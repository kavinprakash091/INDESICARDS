A Desi card games - Indian monuments

The primary motive of this game to spread knowledge of Indian monuments to all age group people.

Methodology

  • Framing of game structure includes overall layout of game.
  • Framing of card involves the determining of factors, structure and components in the card.
  • In Layout design the design language of the card for suitable theme is chosen.
  • Primary product is prototyped and circulated among various age groups and assessed.
  • Finally, the product is optimized based on feedback and is mass produced.

How to Play?

Step 1

Distribute al the cards evenly to all the players.

Step 2

Each player has to hold the deck of their cards upside down.

Step 3

All players have to flip the uppermost card and play with it.

Step 4

One player raises a c√©rtain factor in his/her card and all the players has to play with that factor in that round. The player with the significant number* wins the cards of other players.
For example: Player 1 has Brihadeshwara temple card and he claims the age of it and player 2 and player 3 has cards with age less that of brihadeshwara temple, they have to give their cards to player.

Step 5

The player who won the previous round, gets the opportunity to start the next round.

Step 6

The player with all the cards wins the game.

  • When the factor between all the players gets dashed, the players have to place the cards in the centre and the one who wins with his upcoming card gets all the cards placed under clash.

Layout of Card

The layout of the desi card is based on simple layout and the following factors were considered in deciding the layout:

  • Size of the card - Bridge size card (57mm x 88.8mm)
  • Composition of the card
  • Font style - Kritsen ITC and Mistral
  • Colour composition - Gold and Black colour palette
  • Card Type - Coreless
  • Card Box type

About us

  • Desi card game is a card game which is completely based on the Indian monuments.
  • The term 'Desi' which means 'local/indigenous' is selected as the title of the game.
  • India + Desi = inDesi.
  • Primary motive: Spread knowledge of Indian monuments to all age group people.

Statement

  • Existing games: Focused on cricket, wrestling, number and merely fun.
  • Lack of knowledge and awareness about Indian monuments among, not only students, but Indians in majority.
  • Need of a different route in the approach of learning.

Solution

  • Simple yet innovative gameplay with source of buildings.
  • InDesi cards, with pictorial representation of buildings help people memorise easily.
  • This card takes an unique route in way of learning.

Uniqueness

  • Only card game based on Indian monuments.
  • Education through games.
  • A versatile product for various purposes such as entertainment, education, competitive exams, etc
